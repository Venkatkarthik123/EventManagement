Kanyadana

Description:

Kanyadanam, or "giving away the bride," is a significant Hindu wedding ritual where the father of the bride places his daughter's hand in the groom's hand, symbolizing the transfer of responsibility and blessings for the marriage. This ceremony marks the emotional and spiritual commitment of the families involved. Significance of Kanyadanam Kanyadanam is a revered ritual in Hindu weddings, symbolizing the transfer of responsibility from the bride's parents to the groom. It represents the emotional bond between families and the acceptance of the groom as a new member of the bride's family. The act is seen as a meritorious deed, bringing spiritual benefits to the bride's family..
