Wedding Ceremony

Description:

A Hindu wedding is a vibrant and elaborate affair, rich in traditions and rituals that symbolize the union of two individuals and their families. The ceremony typically spans several days and includes various pre-wedding, wedding, and post-wedding rituals.

Pre-Wedding Rituals:

Engagement (Sagai/Mangni):

A formal commitment between the bride and groom, often involving the exchange of rings and blessings from family members.

Haldi Ceremony:

A ritual where turmeric paste is applied to the bride and groom to bless them and enhance their beauty. This ceremony is usually held a day or two before the wedding.

Mehendi Ceremony:

The bride's hands and feet are adorned with intricate henna designs. This ceremony is often accompanied by music and dance, celebrating the bride's beauty.
